Copyright and Privacy


Research and discussion about copyright & privacy.

Copyright

According to Copyright Licensing NZ (n.d.), “Copyright is a form of intellectual property right. It gives the person who creates an original work exclusive rights to copy, publish, publicly perform, transmit and adapt their material.”. When developing websites and reaching the stage of publishing it, a web developer can find themselves in a bad situation if breaching any copyright laws. Anything that is published within New Zealand is considered to be copyrighted and the copyright lasts as long as 50 years after the death of the creator. It does not need to be registered or have the copyright symbol(©) present, the laws are automatically applied (Copyright Licensing NZ, n.d.). Internationally, copyright can depend on each countrys own laws but any countries that have international agreements with New Zealand have the same laws applied.

Privacy

Privacy is extremely important when it comes to developing and publishing websites as it ensures any user data or personal information is kept safe. The New Zealand Privacy Act (1993) defines how any personal data can be collected, stored, used, accessed and disclosed within New Zealand (data.govt.nz, n.d.). The act contains 12 key principles and is best practice to follow these when developing websites in New Zealand (Privacy Commissioner, 2016).
